Progressive Motorcycle Insurance: How to Avoid Overpaying

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Riding a motorcycle is more than just a mode of transportation—it’s a lifestyle. Whether you’re cruising down coastal highways or navigating urban streets, the thrill of the open road is unmatched. But with great freedom comes great responsibility, and that includes securing the right motorcycle insurance. Progressive is one of the most well-known providers in the U.S., offering competitive rates and customizable policies. However, many riders end up overpaying due to common pitfalls. Here’s how to avoid them.

Why Motorcycle Insurance Costs Are Rising

Inflation and Supply Chain Disruptions

The global economy has been grappling with inflation, and the insurance industry is no exception. Rising costs for motorcycle parts, repairs, and medical services have forced insurers to adjust premiums. Supply chain bottlenecks mean even minor repairs can take longer and cost more, driving up claims expenses.

Increased Theft Rates

Motorcycle thefts have surged in recent years, particularly in urban areas. High-demand models are prime targets, and insurers factor this risk into premiums. If you own a popular bike, you might be paying more simply because of its theft potential.

Climate Change and Extreme Weather

Wildfires, hurricanes, and floods are becoming more frequent, leading to higher comprehensive insurance claims. Even if you live in a low-risk area, insurers spread these costs across all policyholders, contributing to premium hikes.

How Progressive Calculates Your Motorcycle Insurance Rates

Your Riding Profile Matters

Progressive evaluates multiple factors to determine your premium:
- Age and Experience: Younger or less experienced riders typically pay more.
- Riding History: Accidents or traffic violations can significantly increase costs.
- Location: Urban riders often face higher rates due to theft and accident risks.

The Bike You Ride

  • Make and Model: Sport bikes usually cost more to insure than cruisers.
  • Engine Size: Larger engines often mean higher premiums.
  • Modifications: Custom parts can increase replacement costs, raising your rate.

Coverage Choices

  • Liability vs. Full Coverage: Minimum liability is cheaper but offers less protection.
  • Deductibles: Higher deductibles lower premiums but increase out-of-pocket costs during a claim.

5 Ways to Avoid Overpaying for Progressive Motorcycle Insurance

1. Bundle Policies for Maximum Savings

Progressive offers multi-policy discounts. Bundling your motorcycle insurance with auto, home, or renters insurance can save you up to 20%.

2. Take a Motorcycle Safety Course

Completing an approved safety course not only makes you a better rider but can also qualify you for discounts. Progressive recognizes certifications from organizations like the Motorcycle Safety Foundation (MSF).

3. Adjust Your Coverage Seasonally

If you store your bike during winter, consider reducing coverage to comprehensive-only (theft and damage) until riding season resumes. Progressive’s "lay-up" option can lower premiums during inactive months.

4. Improve Your Credit Score

In most states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores to assess risk. A higher score can lead to lower rates. Paying bills on time and reducing debt can help.

5. Shop Around Annually

Loyalty doesn’t always pay. Compare Progressive’s rates with competitors like Geico, Dairyland, or Nationwide every year to ensure you’re getting the best deal.

Hidden Discounts You Might Be Missing

Motorcycle Association Memberships

Belonging to groups like the American Motorcyclist Association (AMA) or Harley Owners Group (HOG) can unlock exclusive discounts.

Anti-Theft Devices

Installing a GPS tracker, alarm, or wheel lock can reduce theft risk and qualify you for savings.

Low-Mileage Discounts

If you’re a casual rider, Progressive may offer lower rates for bikes with limited annual mileage.

Common Mistakes That Inflate Your Premium

Overestimating Your Bike’s Value

Insuring your motorcycle for its original purchase price rather than its current market value can lead to unnecessary costs. Use tools like Kelley Blue Book to assess accurate worth.

Skipping Gap Insurance for New Bikes

If you finance a new motorcycle, gap insurance covers the difference between the loan balance and the bike’s depreciated value after a total loss. Without it, you could owe thousands out of pocket.

Ignoring Small Claims

Filing multiple small claims can label you as high-risk, increasing future premiums. For minor repairs, paying out-of-pocket might be cheaper long-term.

The Future of Motorcycle Insurance

Telematics and Usage-Based Policies

Progressive’s Snapshot program tracks riding habits via a mobile app or device. Safe riders can earn discounts based on real-time data, a trend likely to grow.

Electric Motorcycles and New Risks

As EVs like Harley-Davidson’s LiveWire gain popularity, insurers will adjust policies for battery-related risks and specialized repair costs.

Regulatory Changes

States are increasingly mandating higher liability minimums, which could raise baseline premiums. Staying informed helps you adapt your coverage proactively.

By understanding these factors and leveraging discounts, you can enjoy the ride without overpaying for protection. Stay safe, stay savvy, and keep the rubber side down.
