Kemper Insurance for Non-Standard Drivers: High-Risk Solutions

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Let’s be honest: driving isn’t just a convenience anymore; it’s a lifeline. It’s how we get to work, take our kids to school, and maintain our independence. But for a significant portion of drivers—those labeled “non-standard” or “high-risk”—this essential part of life comes with a heavy price tag and a side of anxiety. In today’s world, where economic pressures are mounting, climate change is altering our physical landscape, and technology is reshaping everything, finding affordable and reliable auto insurance feels like an impossible quest.

This is where companies like Kemper step in. While many insurers compete for the perfect driver with a spotless record, Kemper has carved out a significant niche by offering solutions for those who don’t fit the standard mold. For drivers navigating a high-risk classification, understanding the options available is the first step toward regaining control and financial stability.

Who is the "Non-Standard" Driver in Today's Context?

The term “non-standard” can feel like a cold, corporate label. But in reality, it encompasses a wide range of everyday people facing unique circumstances. The definition has also expanded to include modern-day challenges.

The Traditional High-Risk Profile

This is the profile most people think of. It includes drivers with: * A History of Violations: Multiple speeding tickets, reckless driving charges, or, most seriously, a DUI/DWI conviction. * At-Fault Accidents: A record of collisions where you were deemed responsible. * Lapses in Coverage: Periods where you did not have active auto insurance, which insurers see as a significant risk. * New Drivers: Young, inexperienced drivers, typically teenagers, who lack a driving history for insurers to assess.

The Modern High-Risk Driver: New World, New Challenges

The past few years have introduced new factors that can place drivers in a non-standard category, often through no direct fault of their own. * The Economic Squeeze: Following global economic disruptions, many people faced job losses or reduced income. Letting insurance lapse wasn’t a choice about risk; it was a financial necessity. Now, as they recover, they are penalized for that lapse. * Climate Change and Catastrophic Claims: If you live in an area now frequently hit by wildfires, hurricanes, or severe flooding (think California, Florida, or Louisiana), you may be deemed a higher risk simply because of your ZIP code. A higher concentration of comprehensive claims from weather events drives up rates for everyone in the region. * The Tech Paradox: Modern cars are equipped with advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) like automatic emergency braking and lane-keeping assist, which are designed to prevent accidents. However, when they do get into accidents, the cost of repairing sensors and cameras is exorbitantly high. Drivers of these newer vehicles might see higher premiums because the potential cost of a claim is so much greater. * Gig Economy Drivers: The rise of Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, and Instacart has created a new class of drivers who use their personal vehicles for commercial purposes. Standard personal auto policies often don’t cover this activity, creating a coverage gap that places these drivers in a high-risk category if not properly insured.

Kemper's High-Risk Insurance Solutions: A Closer Look

Kemper Corporation, through its Kemper Auto division, offers a suite of products specifically designed for non-standard drivers. Their approach isn’t about denying coverage; it’s about managing risk through tailored policies and programs.

1. Personalized Policy Underwriting

Unlike companies that use a one-size-fits-all algorithm, Kemper employs more personalized underwriting for its non-standard book of business. This means an agent might take the time to understand the story behind a DUI or a coverage lapse. Was it a single mistake from years ago? Was the lapse due to a documented financial hardship? This nuanced approach can sometimes lead to more favorable outcomes than a fully automated online quote system.

2. Kemper's Flex Term Pay-Per-Mile Insurance

This is a potentially revolutionary solution for a specific type of high-risk driver: the low-mileage driver. Many people with a blemish on their record are also those who drive infrequently—perhaps they work from home or use public transportation for their commute. Kemper’s Flex Term policy combines a low base rate with a per-mile charge. You only pay for the miles you actually drive, tracked through a mobile app or a plug-in device. For a driver trying to rebuild their record, this offers a way to save money and demonstrate low-risk behavior by simply driving less. It directly addresses both the financial and behavioral aspects of being high-risk.

3. Defensive Driving Discounts

Kemper actively encourages policyholders to improve their driving records. One of the most straightforward ways to do this is by completing an accredited defensive driving course. Upon completion, drivers can often qualify for a discount on their premiums. This isn’t just a savings mechanism; it’s a proactive tool for risk reduction. It helps drivers develop safer habits, which can prevent future accidents and violations, ultimately moving them out of the high-risk category faster.

4. Standard High-Risk Coverage Options

Of course, Kemper also provides the essential coverage that non-standard drivers need to meet state legal requirements and protect their assets: * SR-22 and FR-44 Filings: These are not insurance policies themselves but certificates that prove you carry the state-mandated minimum liability insurance. They are required after serious offenses like DUIs or driving without insurance. Kemper is experienced in handling these filings and can efficiently manage the process with the state DMV on your behalf. * Standard Liability, Comprehensive, and Collision: They offer the full range of coverage options, allowing you to build a policy that fits your needs, even if your risk profile is elevated.

Beyond the Policy: Strategies for Managing High-Risk Status

Getting a policy from Kemper or another non-standard carrier is the first step. The ultimate goal is to improve your standing and transition back to standard insurance with lower rates.

Embrace Telematics (Cautiously)

Usage-based insurance (UBI) programs, like Kemper’s own Signal app, monitor your driving habits—braking, speed, time of day, and mileage. For a high-risk driver confident in their ability to drive safely now, this can be a golden opportunity. Demonstrating consistent safe driving over a period of 6-12 months can lead to significant discounts and prove to any future insurer that you are no longer a high risk. Proceed with caution, however, as poor driving data could potentially have a negative impact.

The Long Game: Patience and Consistency

Most violations and accidents lose their impact on your premium over time. A DUI might affect your rates for 3-5 years, while a minor speeding ticket might only affect them for 3. The key is to drive clean during this period. Consistent, violation-free driving is the most powerful factor in repairing your insurance record.

Shop Smart at Renewal

Don’t assume your first high-risk policy is your permanent fate. Your relationship with a non-standard insurer should be a transitional one. Every six to twelve months, you should get new quotes. Once violations begin to age off your record, you may be surprised to find that you qualify for standard rates again. Kemper itself has standard insurance divisions, and they may offer to move you internally as your risk profile improves.

The road for non-standard drivers is undoubtedly tougher, filled with higher costs and more scrutiny. However, it is not a dead end. Insurers like Kemper provide a necessary service, offering a path forward when others shut the door. In an era defined by economic uncertainty, climate-related disruptions, and technological evolution, the very definition of risk is changing. By understanding the options available, leveraging technology, and committing to safer driving, high-risk drivers can navigate this challenging landscape and eventually find their way back to smoother, more affordable roads. The journey requires patience and smart choices, but the destination—financial relief and peace of mind—is well worth the effort.
