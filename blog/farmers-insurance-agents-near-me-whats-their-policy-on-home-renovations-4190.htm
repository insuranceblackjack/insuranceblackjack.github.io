Farmers Insurance Agents Near Me: What’s Their Policy on Home Renovations?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Home renovations are more than just a trend—they’re a necessity in today’s ever-changing housing market. Whether you’re upgrading your kitchen, adding a home office, or reinforcing your property against climate risks, understanding how your insurance policy covers these changes is crucial. Farmers Insurance agents near you can provide tailored guidance, but what exactly should homeowners know before breaking ground?

Why Home Renovations Impact Your Insurance

Home improvements can significantly alter your property’s value and risk profile. A new roof may lower premiums, while a swimming pool could increase liability exposure. Farmers Insurance, like many providers, adjusts policies based on renovations. Here’s what you need to consider:

1. Types of Renovations That Affect Coverage

  • Structural Changes (Additions, Roofing, Foundations): These often increase rebuild costs, requiring policy updates.
  • High-End Upgrades (Luxury Kitchens, Smart Home Tech): Standard policies may not fully cover premium materials.
  • Safety Improvements (Storm-Proofing, Fire-Resistant Materials): Some upgrades qualify for discounts.

2. When to Notify Your Farmers Agent

Don’t wait until after renovations are done. Contact your agent:
- Before Major Projects to avoid gaps in coverage.
- During Permitting to ensure compliance with local laws.
- After Completion to adjust your policy’s dwelling coverage.

Climate Change and Renovation Risks: A Growing Concern

With wildfires, floods, and hurricanes intensifying, Farmers Insurance has adapted policies to address climate-related risks. For example:

Wildfire Zones

  • Mitigation Measures: Installing ember-resistant vents or fireproof siding may lower premiums.
  • Coverage Limits: Some high-risk areas require endorsements.

Flood-Prone Regions

  • Standard Policies Exclude Floods: Separate flood insurance (often via NFIP) is needed.
  • Elevation Upgrades: Raising your home’s foundation could reduce costs.

The Role of Farmers Agents in Customizing Your Policy

Local Farmers agents excel at personalizing coverage. Key questions to ask:
- "Does my policy cover contractor accidents?" (Liability clauses matter.)
- "Are temporary living expenses included if renovations make my home uninhabitable?"
- "How do green upgrades (solar panels, energy-efficient windows) affect my rates?"

Real-Life Scenarios: When Renovations Led to Claims

Case 1: The Unpermitted Garage Conversion

A homeowner added a bedroom without permits. After a fire, Farmers denied the claim due to non-compliance. Lesson: Always disclose renovations.

Case 2: Post-Hurricane Upgrades

A Florida family installed impact-resistant windows. Their agent updated the policy, reducing premiums by 12%.

Final Tips for Homeowners

  • Document Everything: Keep receipts, permits, and before/after photos.
  • Review Annually: Renovations change your home’s value—adjust coverage accordingly.
  • Compare Quotes: Some renovations (like security systems) may qualify for multi-policy discounts.

Farmers Insurance agents near you are a call away—leveraging their expertise ensures your dream renovation doesn’t become an insurance nightmare.
