Farmers Insurance Roadside Assistance: How to Report an Issue

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When you’re stranded on the side of the road with a flat tire, a dead battery, or an empty gas tank, knowing how to quickly access roadside assistance can be a lifesaver. Farmers Insurance offers reliable roadside assistance to its policyholders, but in today’s fast-paced world, understanding how to efficiently report an issue is crucial. With rising fuel costs, extreme weather events, and increasing vehicle technology complexities, being prepared for roadside emergencies has never been more important.

Why Roadside Assistance Matters in Today’s World

The Impact of Climate Change on Vehicle Breakdowns

Extreme weather conditions—from scorching heatwaves to polar vortexes—are putting additional strain on vehicles. Batteries die faster in extreme cold, while overheating engines become more common during heatwaves. Farmers Insurance roadside assistance helps drivers navigate these challenges, but knowing how to quickly report an issue ensures faster response times when you need help the most.

Rising Fuel Prices and the Push for Electric Vehicles (EVs)

As gas prices fluctuate and more drivers switch to EVs, new roadside challenges emerge. Running out of charge in an EV is different from running out of gas, and not all tow trucks are equipped to handle electric vehicles. Farmers Insurance adapts to these changes, but policyholders must understand how to request the right kind of assistance.

How to Report a Roadside Issue with Farmers Insurance

Step 1: Confirm Your Coverage

Before you find yourself in an emergency, review your policy to confirm that roadside assistance is included. Some policies may have it as an add-on, while others include it as a standard feature. Log in to your Farmers account or check your policy documents to verify your coverage.

Step 2: Contact Farmers Roadside Assistance

Once you confirm coverage, reporting an issue is straightforward. You have multiple options:

Option 1: Call the 24/7 Hotline

The fastest way to get help is by calling Farmers’ dedicated roadside assistance hotline. Keep this number saved in your phone for quick access. A representative will ask for:
- Your policy number
- Your location (use GPS or landmarks)
- The nature of your issue (flat tire, dead battery, lockout, etc.)

Option 2: Use the Farmers Mobile App

For tech-savvy drivers, the Farmers mobile app allows you to request assistance with just a few taps. The app can even share your real-time location for faster service.

Option 3: Submit a Request Online

If you’re in a safe location and prefer digital communication, you can log in to your Farmers account and submit a roadside assistance request through their website.

Step 3: Provide Necessary Details

Whether you call, use the app, or submit a request online, be prepared to give:
- Your vehicle’s make, model, and color
- License plate number
- A description of the problem
- Any special circumstances (e.g., you’re in a dangerous area, have pets in the car, etc.)

Step 4: Wait for Assistance and Follow Instructions

Once your request is processed, Farmers will dispatch a service provider to your location. Depending on your issue, this could be a tow truck, a locksmith, or a jump-start technician. Stay in a safe place and follow any instructions given by the dispatcher.

Common Roadside Emergencies and How Farmers Can Help

Flat Tires

With potholes worsening due to extreme weather, flat tires are a frequent issue. Farmers’ roadside assistance includes tire changes if you have a spare. If not, they can arrange a tow to the nearest repair shop.

Dead Batteries

Whether it’s due to cold weather or leaving lights on, battery issues are common. Farmers provides jump-start services to get you back on the road quickly.

Lockouts

Locking your keys in the car is frustrating, but Farmers can send a locksmith to help you regain access without damaging your vehicle.

Running Out of Fuel (or Charge)

If you’re out of gas, Farmers can deliver enough fuel to get you to the nearest station. For EVs, they may arrange a tow to a charging station if mobile charging isn’t available.

Mechanical Breakdowns

If your car won’t start or has a major mechanical failure, Farmers can arrange a tow to a nearby repair facility covered under your policy.

Tips for Staying Safe While Waiting for Help

  • Pull Over Safely: If possible, move your vehicle to the shoulder or a parking lot away from traffic.
  • Use Hazard Lights: Make your car visible to other drivers, especially at night or in bad weather.
  • Stay Inside Your Vehicle: Unless you’re in immediate danger (e.g., on a busy highway), it’s often safer to stay inside with doors locked.
  • Have Emergency Supplies: Keep water, a flashlight, and a first-aid kit in your car in case of long wait times.

The Future of Roadside Assistance

As technology evolves, so does roadside assistance. Farmers Insurance is exploring innovations like:
- AI-Powered Dispatch Systems: Faster, more accurate service assignments based on real-time data.
- EV-Specific Services: More mobile charging units and specialized technicians for electric vehicles.
- Predictive Assistance: Using vehicle telematics to detect potential issues before they leave you stranded.

By staying informed and knowing how to report an issue efficiently, you can make the most of Farmers Insurance roadside assistance and stay prepared for whatever the road throws your way.
